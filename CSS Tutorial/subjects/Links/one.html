Fun Facts About Guitars

  1. The word "guitar" comes from the Spanish word "guitarra" which itself comes from the Arabic word "qitara" meaning "stringed instrument".
  2. The world's largest functioning guitar was built by students at the Academy of Science and Technology in Texas in 2001. It measures 43 feet and 7.5 inches and weighs over 2,000 pounds.
  3. The earliest known guitar-like instrument dates back to ancient Egypt and was discovered in a tomb painting from around 1425 BC.
  4. The first electric guitar was invented in 1931 by George Beauchamp and Adolph Rickenbacker of the Ro-Pat-In Corporation (later renamed Electro String Instrument Corporation).
  5. The Beatles' 1965 hit "Ticket to Ride" features a guitar riff played by George Harrison that was inspired by the sound of the clattering of a train.

History of Guitars

The history of guitars can be traced back to ancient times, with stringed instruments like the oud and the lute being the predecessors of the modern guitar. The word guitar itself comes from the Spanish word "guitarra", which is derived from the Arabic word "qitara". The modern six-string guitar that we know today began to take shape in the 16th century in Spain, with many different types and variations being developed over time. In the late 1800s, steel strings were introduced, which allowed for greater volume and projection. The first electric guitar was invented in the 1930s and paved the way for an entire new era of musical styles and genres. Today, guitars come in a wide range of styles and types, including acoustic, electric, classical, and many more.
